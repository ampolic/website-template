If your lawn is thin, patchy, or feels like concrete in August, the problem is usually below the surface. Core aeration pulls thousands of small soil plugs, letting air, water, and nutrients reach the roots — and it is the perfect seedbed for overseeding.

## What We Do

- Double-pass core aeration with commercial machines
- Premium seed blend matched to your sun and soil conditions
- Starter fertilizer to push fast germination
- Marking of irrigation heads and invisible fences before we start
- Simple watering instructions left at your door

## When To Do It

Early fall is the ideal window in central Illinois: warm soil, cool nights, and fewer weeds competing with new seedlings. Spring aeration is available for lawns that can't wait, but fall gives the best results.

## What To Expect After

The soil plugs break down within two to three weeks and act as a topdressing. New seedlings appear in 10–21 days with proper watering. By the following spring, treated lawns are noticeably thicker and hold up far better to summer heat.

## Good Candidates

- Lawns with visible thinning or bare patches
- Heavy clay soil or high-traffic areas
- Any lawn older than a few years that has never been aerated
