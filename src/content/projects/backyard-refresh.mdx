The homeowners bought this property knowing the backyard needed work: shrubs that hadn't been touched in a decade, beds with no defined edges, and a lawn worn thin by shade and foot traffic. They wanted a space their kids could use that wouldn't demand every weekend to maintain.

## Scope of Work

- Removed twelve overgrown shrubs and two truckloads of accumulated debris
- Cut new bed lines and installed steel hardscape edging throughout
- Planted native, drought-resistant perennials selected for part shade
- Applied fresh cedar mulch across roughly 900 square feet of beds
- Core-aerated, leveled, and overseeded the lawn with a shade-tolerant blend

## Results

Three weeks in, the overseeded areas had filled to a uniform stand, and the new beds gave the yard structure it never had. The plant palette was chosen to fill in over two growing seasons with almost no pruning — exactly the low-maintenance space the family asked for. They've since moved to our weekly mowing plan to keep it that way.
