This half-acre lot had been mowed but never fed. By the time the owners called, the "lawn" was closer to 60% crabgrass and bare soil than turf. Rather than strip and re-sod at several times the cost, we proposed a one-season renovation.

## Scope of Work

- Soil test to identify pH and nutrient deficiencies
- Selective herbicide program through late summer to knock down weeds
- Double-pass core aeration in early September
- Overseeded with a premium tall fescue and bluegrass blend at renovation rate
- Starter fertilizer at seeding, winterizer in late November
- Enrolled in the six-step fertilization program for the following season

## Results

By the first mow of the following spring, the stand was dense enough that the owners' neighbors asked who re-sodded the lawn. Total cost came in at roughly a third of sod replacement, and the soil corrections mean the results will hold.
