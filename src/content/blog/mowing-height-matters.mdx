If we could change one habit for every homeowner in Springfield, it would be this: raise your mower deck. Scalping the lawn to stretch time between mows is the most common self-inflicted lawn injury we see, and it causes problems that fertilizer can't fix.

## The One-Third Rule

Never remove more than one-third of the grass blade in a single mow. Cut a 4.5-inch lawn down to 3 inches and the plant shrugs it off. Cut a 6-inch lawn down to 2 inches and you've removed two-thirds of its ability to photosynthesize — the plant responds by burning root reserves to regrow leaf tissue, which weakens it just as summer stress arrives.

## What Short Mowing Actually Costs You

- **More weeds.** Sunlight reaching the soil is exactly what crabgrass seed is waiting for. Tall, dense turf is your best pre-emergent.
- **More watering.** Short grass has shallow roots. Shallow roots dry out first.
- **More disease.** Stressed turf is where fungus gets its start.

## Our Height Recommendations

| Season    | Cool-season lawns (fescue, bluegrass) |
| --------- | ------------------------------------- |
| Spring    | 3 inches                              |
| Summer    | 3.5 – 4 inches                        |
| Fall      | 3 inches                              |
| Final mow | 2.5 inches                            |

## And Keep the Blade Sharp

A dull blade tears grass instead of cutting it, leaving frayed, whitish tips that lose moisture and invite disease. Sharpen at least twice a season — our crews sharpen daily. If your lawn looks slightly gray-white two days after mowing, the blade is the reason.
